Baby Turtle Survival

Play Game

Overview of Prototype

A side-scrolling game for kids ages 8-11 where players control a turtle navigating obstacles that represent real-world dangers, with educational messages shown upon failure. The prototype includes basic movement, hazards such as oil can, paper bag, crabs, seagulls, and cars, informative death messages, and a power-up that clears obstacles to highlight environmental impact.

Educational Learning Goals

Teaches the challenges of a baby turtle's survival journey, emphasizing real threats like predators and pollution through contextual, behaviorist learning. Uses negative reinforcement with death and positive reinforcement with clean-up actions to promote environmental awareness and conservation behavior.

Playtesting Feedback

Players found the game engaging and educational, but suggested improvements to onboarding, clearer visuals, and more realistic baby turtle experience. Feedback highlighted the need for better clarity of hazards, added guidance, health mechanics, and enhanced audio and visual cues to improve learning and gameplay experience.

Game Mechanics

A 2D side-scroller with simple movement: left, right, and jump. Features an energy bar for urgency and hazards like crabs, oil spills, obstructing cars, and seagulls that instantly kill the player. Includes a balancing power-up, the beach ball, that clears obstacles, reinforcing gameplay fairness while highlighting environmental impact.

Game Iterations

After playtesting, we improved sound design by adding effects for jumping, dying, and winning, along with background music that matches the environment. We enhanced item feedback using pulsing glows, with white indicating positive effects and red indicating negative ones. To boost playability, we added a speedrun timer and high score tracker. We expanded the course by 1.5 times and enabled fullscreen gameplay to improve length and visibility. We emphasized the challenge of baby turtle survival, noting that only 1 in 1000 survive, and added a hatching animation for context. To balance difficulty, we slowed energy drain, reduced crab numbers, and shrank the turtle hitbox while making food harder to find.
